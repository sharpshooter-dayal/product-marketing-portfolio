217% Growth in Inbound Leads for a B2B SaaS Data Company

Digital Divide Data

Problem

Solution

Impact
  • 217% growth in inbound leads attributable to organic content within 9 months
  • 38 keywords ranked on page 1 of Google
  • 31% of new pipeline sourced from organic by end of program
  • 14 of 22 target publications featured DDD content or mentions
Lifecycle & Automation

34% of Nurtured Leads Converted to Sales Calls Within 30 Days

Digital Divide Data

Problem

No structured nurture flow existed for inbound leads. Sales had no consistent way to move new contacts toward a sales conversation.

Solution
  • Segmented leads by vertical (Robotics, Healthcare AI, ADAS) and by content engagement source
  • Built automated email sequences triggered by lead behavior, not just time-based drips
  • Wrote vertical-specific case studies for qualified leads
  • Added a clear, low-pressure CTA at each stage building toward a discovery call
  • Set up lead scoring thresholds to flag sales-ready contacts automatically
Impact
  • 34% of nurtured leads booked a sales call within 30 days
  • Reduced manual follow-up workload for sales by an estimated 8 hours per week
  • Improved lead-to-opportunity conversion rate by 21%
  • Cut average time from lead capture to first sales touch from 6 days to under 24 hours
Positioning & Paid

15% More Qualified Leads Through ICP-Led Ad Repositioning

Shotova

Problem

Existing Google Ads messaging targeted a broad, generic SaaS buyer. CTR and lead quality were both underperforming.

Solution
  • Defined a sharper ICP, the “overwhelmed team lead,” based on customer interviews and activation data
  • Rewrote ad copy to lead with the specific pain point this persona used in their own words
  • Matched landing page headlines and structure directly to ad messaging to eliminate post-click drop-off
  • Removed generic feature-led copy in favor of outcome-led language
  • A/B tested 3 headline variants before scaling spend to the winning version
Impact
  • 19% increase in click-through rate
  • 15% increase in qualified leads with no increase in spend
  • 12% reduction in cost per qualified lead
  • Improved Quality Score, lowering average CPC across the campaign
Content Architecture

314% Organic Traffic Growth Through a 4-Pillar Content Strategy

Shotova

Problem

Near-zero organic visibility. The SaaS company was entirely dependent on paid acquisition with rising CAC.

Solution
  • Built 4 content pillars around buyer pain points (async communication, remote team productivity) instead of product features
  • Published 18 supporting cluster pages with intentional internal linking back to each pillar
  • Audited 6 competitors to identify content gaps and avoid duplicating saturated topics
  • Added FAQ schema and structured answers to improve visibility in AI-generated search results
  • Fixed technical SEO issues (page speed, crawl errors, schema markup) to support the content investment
Impact
  • 314% growth in organic traffic in 12 months
  • Organic-attributed signups grew to 31% of total new signups
  • 41 keywords ranked on page 1 of Google, up from 3
  • Reduced blended CAC as paid dependency dropped
Enterprise Paid Media

28% Improvement in Lead Quality for a Global Technology Consultancy

VE3, Technology Consulting & Digital Transformation

Problem

VE3’s broad service portfolio (AI, Cloud, Data, Enterprise Platforms) made it hard to target a single high-intent audience. Generic campaigns produced low-quality leads.

Solution
  • Segmented Google Ads and LinkedIn campaigns by 3 priority service lines: AI Solutions, Cloud Engineering, Enterprise Platforms
  • Built dedicated landing pages per service line instead of routing all traffic to one generic page
  • Tailored ad copy and offers to the specific buyer evaluating that service line
  • Layered in negative keywords to filter out low-intent, informational searches
  • Reviewed campaign performance weekly and reallocated budget toward the highest-converting service line
Impact
  • 28% improvement in lead quality, measured by sales-qualified rate
  • 22% reduction in cost per qualified lead
  • Higher campaign efficiency allowed budget to be reallocated toward best-performing service lines without increasing total spend
